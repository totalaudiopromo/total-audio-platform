Lydia the Bard - Grow Up

Lydia the Bard's Alternative Rock track, Grow Up, draws powerful inspiration from Tourist, Madeon, M83, The Chemical Brothers, & Friendly Fires.

Listen & Download for radio

Taylor Nelson is a multi-instrumentalist, vocalist, and Alternative Rock producer who goes by the stage name Lydia the Bard. His brand-new single ‘Grow Up’ serves as the opening track from debut forthcoming album, CIRCUMVOLVE.


Having taken a hiatus after his last project and focusing on travel photography as his main creative outlet, the onset of the COVID pandemic and localised lockdowns brought Taylor back to creating music again. Blending buzzing synths, big beats, and soaring vocals, Lydia the Bard draws from influences across many genres and past experiences in groups like Wiping Out Thousands, Tired Tongues, and The New Monarchs.


Produced, mixed, and engineered at a home studio, the Alternative Rocka offering has an energetic and hopeful production that features guitar, drums, bass, synths, and drum machines. The vibrant production of ‘Grow Up’ is paired with captivating vocals that will draw the listener in immediately.


‘Grow Up’ is an acknowledgment of the banal that dominated our lives while we waited for the COVID pandemic to end being cooped up in our houses. The lockdowns extended into an ephemeral time as time went on. The track alludes to the feelings we all experienced during these uncertain times—that life would ultimately return to normal and that we all needed to have a deeper appreciation for what we have and take for granted—as we stared at the same four walls, day after day, yearning for a change.


After the global pandemic of COVID-19 struck, Lydia the Bard soon realised that he needed a creative outlet. He started writing again after years without picking up an instrument in an attempt to reestablish routine. This soon led to the setup of a new studio at home and the recording of numerous demos and ideas. He eventually became tired of staring at his house's four walls and understood that he desperately needed eye therapy. Thus was born the lead single off his debut album. With no sign of a permanent end to the lockdown, he wondered if he would ever be able to view the world he loves in the same way. ‘Grow Up’ set the tone and direction for where the rest of this debut album would venture off to.


The single is accompanied by a music video. Justin Shen's work on Instagram was discovered by Lydia the Bard in the summer of 2023. He was immediately drawn to his hyper-lapse filming, especially the more stuttering frame-lapse components. He had always known he wanted to make a music video for ‘Grow Up’, and Justin's approach, which emphasises recurring patterns and similarities between shapes, went so well with the song's message about being stuck at home and staring at the same shapes all day long in the hopes of finding a way out.

“‘Grow Up’ started as the first demo track I began working on under the moniker Lydia the Bard — though it wasn't the first track to be completed, it became the best possible introduction to—and summation of—what Lydia the Bard is.”

- Lydia the Bard
