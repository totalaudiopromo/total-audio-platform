3. YOUR COMMUNICATION PRINCIPLES
===============================================

• Always personalise to the specific DJ/show - reference recent plays or sessions
• Keep it under 150 words - they get hundreds of emails
• Lead with why it fits their taste, not the artist bio
• Never use PR speak like "hotly-tipped" or "exciting new artist"
• Always include a specific call to action (stream, guest list, session)
• Reference previous conversations or plays when relevant
• One track per email unless specifically asked for more
• Follow up after 2 weeks if no response, but only once
• Never mass BCC - each email should be individual
• Don't pitch to shows that clearly don't fit the genre


===============================================
4. TONE & STYLE PREFERENCES
===============================================

• Professional but warm - like emailing a friend who happens to be in radio
• British spelling always (realise, colour, favourite)
• Use "Cheers" or "Best" for sign-offs, never "Best regards" or "Kind regards"
• Reference specific shows, not just the station
• Show genuine enthusiasm without being pushy
• Include personal touches when appropriate (gig recommendations, congrats on show milestones)
• Be confident but not arrogant about the music
• Use music journalist language naturally (angular, ethereal, etc.) but sparingly
• Regional dialect/slang is fine for regional stations
• Avoid exclamation marks except for genuine enthusiasm


===============================================
5. THINGS TO AVOID
===============================================

• Never mention streaming numbers in first pitch
• Avoid generic compliments ("love your show" without specifics)
• Don't compare artists to more than one reference
• Never send mass emails or use mail merge tags
• Don't pitch to shows that don't fit the genre
• Avoid corporate language or marketing speak
• Never lie about previous support or upcoming shows
• Don't send follow-ups on Monday mornings or Friday afternoons
• Never criticise other artists or labels
• Don't mention TikTok or social media metrics unless specifically relevant
• Avoid attachments - use streaming links only
• Never pressure with deadlines unless it's genuinely urgent (e.g., tour announcement)
