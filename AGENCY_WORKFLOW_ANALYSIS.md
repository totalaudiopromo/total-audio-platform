## Phase 1: Campaign Planning (Week 1)

### Activities

**Current Workflow:**

1. **Artist Briefing** (1-2 hours)

## Phase 2: Contact Research (Weeks 1-2) üî¥ CRITICAL PAIN POINT

### Current Manual Process

**Radio Contact Research (8-10 hours per campaign):**

1. **Identify Target Shows** (2 hours)
   - Research BBC Radio schedule
   - Identify relevant shows (genre, artist level)
   - Jack Saunders (Future Sounds), Clara Amfo, Sian Eleri (Future Artists)
   - Regional BBC stations (BBC Introducing)
   - Specialist shows (6 Music, 1Xtra, etc.)

2. **Find Contact Details** (4-6 hours)
   - Google searches: "[Presenter name] BBC Radio contact email"
   - Check BBC website (often outdated)
   - LinkedIn searches for producers
   - Twitter/Instagram DM attempts
   - Ask industry contacts for introductions

3. **Research Submission Preferences** (2 hours)
   - How does each presenter prefer to receive music?
   - Email? BBC uploader? Physical promo?
   - What format? MP3? WAV? Streaming link?
   - What information do they want in the pitch?

4. **Build Contact Database** (1-2 hours)
   - Enter into spreadsheet
   - Organize by show, genre, priority
   - Add notes on each contact

**Playlist Contact Research (3-5 hours per campaign):**

1. **Identify Target Playlists** (1 hour)
   - Research Spotify editorial playlists
   - Find independent curator playlists
   - Identify genre-relevant playlists

2. **Find Curator Contacts** (2-3 hours)
   - Many playlists don't list curator contact info
   - Social media detective work
   - Check playlist descriptions for submission info

3. **Research Curator Preferences** (1 hour)
   - What music does this playlist feature?
   - Submission process? SubmitHub? Email? Form?

**Press Contact Research (2-3 hours per campaign):**

1. **Identify Target Publications** (30 min)
   - Music blogs and websites
   - Online magazines
   - Genre-specific outlets

2. **Find Journalist/Editor Details** (1-2 hours)
   - Who covers this genre?
   - Current contact email (often outdated on websites)
   - Preferred pitch topics and angles

3. **Update Contact Database** (30 min)
   - Verify emails still valid
   - Update job titles (journalists move frequently)

**TOTAL CONTACT RESEARCH TIME: 15+ hours per campaign**

## Phase 3: Pitch Creation (Week 2) üî¥ CRITICAL PAIN POINT

### Current Manual Process

**Writing Personalised Pitches (2-3 hours per campaign):**

1. **Research Each Contact** (30-60 minutes)
   - Review presenter's recent shows
   - Check Twitter for music they've played
   - Understand their taste and show format
   - Note recent guests or features

2. **Draft Core Pitch** (30 minutes)
   - Artist introduction
   - Music description
   - Why this matters (story, context)
   - Streaming links and assets

3. **Personalise for Each Contact** (60-90 minutes)
   - Custom intro referencing presenter's show
   - Explain why this artist fits their format
   - Mention recent artists they've supported
   - Adjust tone for each outlet (BBC R1 vs 6 Music very different)

4. **Proofread and Refine** (15-30 minutes)
   - Check for typos (critical - bad pitch hurts reputation)
   - Ensure all links work
   - Verify correct name spelling (embarrassing mistakes damage relationships)

**TOTAL PITCH CREATION TIME: 2-3 hours per campaign**

**Multiplier Effect:**
- Liberty runs 10 campaigns/month
- Each campaign = 2.5 hours average pitch creation
- Total: **25 hours/month spent writing pitches**

## Workflow Bottlenecks & Friction Points

### Bottleneck #1: Contact Research Creates Campaign Delays

**Problem:**
- Campaign planning complete (Week 1)
- Contact research takes Week 1-2
- Can't start pitching until contacts found
- 1-2 week delay before campaign really begins

**Impact:**
- Release timing critical (artists have specific release dates)
- 2-week delay = less runway for placements
- Pressure on Sam's team to rush research (leads to errors)

**Solution with Audio Intel:**
- Contact research in 30 seconds (Week 1)
- Pitching begins immediately
- Full 8-12 week campaign runway maintained

---

### Bottleneck #2: Pitch Quality vs Speed Trade-off

**Problem:**
- High-quality personalised pitches take 2-3 hours
- Liberty's reputation depends on pitch quality
- Volume pressure (10 campaigns) forces speed
- Speed sacrifices quality ‚Üí fewer placements

**Sam's Dilemma:**
"Do I spend 3 hours writing the perfect pitch for Jack Saunders, or 30 minutes writing a generic pitch so I can move to the next campaign? I know the perfect pitch works better, but I don't have time."

**Solution with Pitch Generator:**
- AI provides 80% complete personalised pitch in 30 seconds
- Sam refines in 15 minutes
- Quality maintained, speed achieved
- Best of both worlds

---

### Bottleneck #3: Campaign Coordination Across 40+ Freelancers

**Problem:**
- Freelancer A contacts Jack Saunders (Monday)
- Freelancer B doesn't know, contacts Jack Saunders again (Wednesday)
- Jack receives duplicate pitch, annoyed
- Liberty's reputation damaged

**Current Solution:**
- Shared Google Sheet

- **Problem:** Sheet isn't updated in real-time, still get duplicates

**Solution with Campaign Tracker:**

### Bottleneck #4: Client Reporting Delays Campaign Wrap-up
