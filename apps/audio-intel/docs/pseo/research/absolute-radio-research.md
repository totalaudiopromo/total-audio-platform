## Common Mistakes Artists Make

### 1. Sending Audio File Attachments Instead of Web-Links
- **Mistake**: Artists email MP3/WAV attachments to selfreleased@absoluteradio.co.uk
- **Reality**: Absolute Radio explicitly requests WEB-LINK ONLY (Dropbox/SoundCloud) - attachments create email issues and may be rejected
- **Solution**: Upload track to Dropbox or SoundCloud, send link (not attachment) to selfreleased@absoluteradio.co.uk
- **Source**: Absolute Radio Sunday Night Music Club submission guidelines

### 2. Wrong Genre Expectations
- **Mistake**: Sending metal, electronic, hip-hop, or commercial pop to alternative/indie station
- **Reality**: Absolute Radio plays "Coldplay to Foo Fighters, Beatles to Bon Jovi" - alternative, indie, rock for 25-44 demographic
- **Solution**: Only submit if your music genuinely fits alternative/indie/rock genre spectrum
- **Source**: Station format and target demographic

### 3. Expecting Multiple Tracks Featured
- **Mistake**: Artists expect all submitted tracks to be played or multiple tracks featured simultaneously
- **Reality**: Sunday Night Music Club features ONE track per month - highly competitive selection
- **Solution**: Submit your strongest single, accept competitive nature of selection process
- **Source**: Sunday Night Music Club submission guidelines

### 4. Targeting Main Playlist Without Sunday Night Music Club First
- **Mistake**: Trying to approach Dave Berry (Breakfast) or main presenters without Sunday Night Music Club track record
- **Reality**: Sunday Night Music Club is the official route for self-released artists - main playlist requires professional plugging or proven success
- **Solution**: Start with Sunday Night Music Club, build credibility, then consider professional radio campaign
- **Source**: Absolute Radio unsigned/self-released artist pathway

### 5. Poor Quality Recordings
- **Mistake**: Submitting low-quality demos or bedroom recordings
- **Reality**: Absolute Radio is professional station competing with major label releases - requires broadcast-quality audio
- **Solution**: Invest in professional recording/mixing/mastering before submitting (submission guidelines request "best quality")
- **Source**: Radio broadcast quality standards

### 6. Over-Complicated Artist Information
- **Mistake**: Sending lengthy biography, press releases, multiple links, excessive information
- **Reality**: Sunday Night Music Club requests "brief info" - Danielle Perry reviews many submissions, concise information stands out
- **Solution**: Keep email brief: artist name, track title, genre, 2-3 sentence description, essential links only
- **Source**: Sunday Night Music Club submission efficiency
